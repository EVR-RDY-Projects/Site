Our Mission

The Challenge

Critical infrastructure faces three major cybersecurity challenges that traditional solutions fail to address effectively.

Underfunding

Most organizations do not allocate or have the funds to properly secure ICS/OT, leaving critical areas under-resourced.

High Cost Solutions

Leading OT/ICS cybersecurity platforms often start out of the range of small organization.

Workforce Shortage

The U.S. faces a cybersecurity workforce gap of 0+ unfilled jobs, with 67% of organizations reporting shortages.

Our Solution

Two lines of effort: T.A.P.S. (Telemetry Acquisition Platform Solution) and TacSOC (Tactical Security Operations Center).

Affordable Protection

TacSOC pools customers to leverage shared resources while empowering analysts with AI endriched alerts and undercuting costs.

Low-Cost Infrastructure

SEER, GHOST and RAMPART replace expensive OT appliances with affordable with opensourced alternatives. T.A.P.S avoids expensive OT sensing solutions .

Scalable Talent Pipeline

TacSOC automates Tier-1, accelerates analysts from Tier-2 to Tier-3 through mentorship, and spins up new Hunter/Killer Teams — multiplying skilled defenders faster than traditional pipelines.

Our Structure

EVR:RDY launches with a for-profit first model, followed by a nonprofit foundation to scale workforce development and community adoption.

EVR:RDY Defensive Operations Group

Our for-profit entity and current prime performer for T.A.P.S. development. Focuses on turning open innovation into production-quality solutions through T.A.P.S. R&D and fielding, TacSOC development and manning, shared services and resource pooling, and talent investment. Revenue enables competitive compensation and sustainable growth while delivering vendor-backed support that organizations need.

EVR:RDY Foundation for Critical Infrastructure Defense

Future nonprofit entity focused on scaling workforce, safety, and community adoption. Will pursue veteran outreach and workforce development, community validation and safety programs, and open ecosystem stewardship. Funded through donations, sponsorships, training revenue, and fee-for-service community programs with coordinated roadmaps via joint release reviews.

The vision: For-profit innovation drives production-quality solutions while nonprofit foundation scales workforce development and community safety — coordinated through shared standards and joint release reviews. Most organizations won’t adopt open source alone because they don’t have the resources or workforce to maintain it — that’s where we step in.

System Components
▼

SCOUT

Active Development

Lightweight Host Telemetry Agent

Production-ready "slow-and-low" monitoring agent with comprehensive state management, event collection, and system monitoring. Designed for minimal footprint on legacy OT/ICS systems.

Status: 75% complete. Core infrastructure and data collection ready. Transport layer and sensor integration in progress.

SEER

PoC

Scalable Endpoint & Environmental Reconnaissance

Low-cost network sensor built on commodity hardware. Captures full packet data and generates Zeek-style logs for forensic analysis.

Status: Functional prototype with local storage and PCAP generation capabilities.

GHOST

PoC

Hardware‑Enforced Data Diode

Physical air-gap solution using modified media converters and fiber optics. Guarantees unidirectional data flow with no possibility of return communication.

Status: Hardware prototypes tested and validated. Cost optimization in progress.

RAMPART

Active Development

Resilient Architecture for Monitoring & Proactive Advanced Response Teams

Data processing and forwarding engine that normalizes telemetry for SIEM ingestion. Enables remote forensic analysis while maintaining OT isolation.

Status: Core functionality implemented. Chronicle integration and workflow automation in development.

Technical Capabilities
▼

AI Integration

Uses advanced AI models to deduplicate and enrich telemetry, map events to MITRE ATT&CK, and automatically dismiss noise.

Result: Analysts focus on true threats, enabling faster detection and resilient defenses.

Resource Pooling

Leverages cloud‑native SIEMs — currently Google Chronicle — to enable license sharing across customers.

Result: Provides enterprise‑grade monitoring without enterprise‑grade costs.

Hunter/Killer Teams

Small, skilled groups blending Tier‑2 and Tier‑3 analysts under a Team Leader. AI takes Tier-1 work off the table, accelerating progression and scaling defense to many sites.

Result: TacSOC is both a defense platform and a training engine.

R.A.I.D. Framework

Recon, Acquire, Interpret, Defend — a systematic approach to threat hunting and defense operations.

Result: Enables small, distributed teams to operate with autonomy and accountability without massive centralized SOCs.
