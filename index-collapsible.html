T.A.P.S. (Telemetry Acquisition Platform Solution)

A suite of open‑source tools being developed to create low‑cost, practical solutions that move critical telemetry from the OT environment into the IT environment for safe ingestion into a SIEM.

SEER Sensor (Scalable Endpoint & Environmental Reconnaissance) POC
Currently in Proof of Concept (POC). SEER provides low‑cost visibility for OT/ICS and IT networks by capturing mirrored traffic, generating Zeek‑style logs, ingesting JSON from endpoints, and storing forensic data locally.
Why it matters: Smaller utilities and DoD‑critical infrastructure sites gain OT visibility without enterprise‑level licensing costs.

GHOST Node / Diode POC
Currently in Proof of Concept (POC). A hardware‑enforced data diode for unidirectional UDP flow, built from inexpensive media converters, switches, and fiber.
Why it matters: Provides a physical security guarantee that data flows only one way — safeguarding OT environments while enabling centralized monitoring.

RAMPART (Resilient Architecture for Monitoring & Proactive Advanced Response Teams) Active Development
Currently in active development. RAMPART bridges the GHOST Diode and the SIEM of choice (currently Google Chronicle). Packages data for SIEM and allows remote PCAP analysis from SEER without breaking OT isolation.
Why it matters: Ensures telemetry can be safely centralized, normalized, and analyzed for proactive monitoring and forensics.

TacSOC (Tactical Security Operations Center)

TacSOC is EVR:RDY's first flagship product: an AI-enforced, OT-focused SOC built for precision threat hunting, incident response, and real defense. Built on top of T.A.P.S., TacSOC reimagines the SOC with a tactical mindset — lean, efficient, and mission‑focused.

Smarter Defense Through AI Integration
Uses advanced AI models to deduplicate and enrich telemetry, map events to MITRE ATT&CK, and automatically dismiss noise.
Why it matters: Analysts focus on true threats, enabling faster detection and resilient defenses.

Resource Pooling for Affordability
Leverages cloud‑native SIEMs — currently Google Chronicle — to enable license sharing across customers.
Why it matters: Provides enterprise‑grade monitoring without enterprise‑grade costs.

Hunter‑Killer Teams — Scaling Through OJT
Small, skilled groups blending Tier‑2 and Tier‑3 analysts under a Team Leader. AI takes Tier-1 work off the table, accelerating progression and scaling defense to many sites.
Why it matters: Accelerates talent growth and creates a force‑multiplying defense structure. TacSOC is both a defense platform and a training engine.

R.A.I.D. Framework (Recon, Acquire, Interpret, Defend)
R — Recon: Define adversary-driven visibility by analyzing the defended network against threat reporting, intelligence, and known adversary TTPs.
A — Acquire: Deploy Drop VMs and passive taps at OT choke points to close visibility gaps without disrupting operations.
I — Interpret: AI triage enriches, deduplicates, maps events to ATT&CK, and clears low-fidelity alerts automatically.
D — Defend: Deliver mission-ready defense packages: containment, OT hardening, and clear reporting.
Why it matters: Enables small, distributed teams to operate with autonomy and accountability without massive centralized SOCs.

Tactical Acquisition Pathway
The backbone of TacSOC: a rugged, simple way to move telemetry out of OT and semi-air-gapped environments without adding risk.
Flow: Drop VM (inside OT) → Tactical Acquisition Pathway (one-way flow) → Collection VM (IT side) → Google Chronicle (AI triage and storage)
Why it matters: This method avoids million-dollar OT gateways and fragile integrations.
